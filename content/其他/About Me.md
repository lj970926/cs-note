
Hi, I’m a developer interested in building fast and reliable software at the intersection of systems engineering and machine learning.

I primarily work with **C++** and **Python**. C++ allows me to explore performance, memory management, concurrency, and runtime behavior, while Python helps me iterate quickly across the machine learning ecosystem. I’m also exploring **Rust** and its approach to safe, expressive systems programming.

My main area of interest is **Machine Learning Systems (MLSys)**, particularly large language model inference and serving. I enjoy digging into projects such as **vLLM** and **SGLang**, learning how techniques like continuous batching, request scheduling, KV-cache management, and distributed execution affect latency, throughput, and memory efficiency.

I also work with **PaddlePaddle** and **PyTorch**, and I’m curious about the entire journey from model definition to efficient production workloads—including runtimes, distributed systems, model serving, and performance optimization.

This blog is where I document what I learn: source-code notes, engineering trade-offs, performance investigations, and experiments that might save someone else a little time. I believe the best way to understand a system is to look beneath its abstractions, measure its behavior, and build something with it.

You can find my projects and experiments on [GitHub](https://github.com/lj970926).
